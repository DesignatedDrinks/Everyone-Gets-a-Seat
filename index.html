The book

This is not a polished redemption story.

A man stops drinking. That is where the easy version of the story ends—and where this book begins.

Everyone Gets a Seat follows what happened after Mike’s family left, after the final beer, and after the promises had to become visible behaviour. Kristen tells the story from the seat that recovery stories too often push to the edge: the person who lived beside the drinking, protected the children, left, and chose for herself whether to return.

Together, Mike and Kristen examine marriage, family, ambition, and the company that grew from their belief that belonging should not depend on what is in your glass.

The authors

Mike & Kristen Norris

Two educators, two perspectives, and one honest account of rebuilding a family.

Kristen Norris has been a teacher for 22 years and holds a Master of Education. Her career has been grounded in helping students grow, creating classrooms where people feel seen, and understanding that compassion and boundaries can exist at the same time.

Mike Norris is a tool and die maker by trade and holds a degree in education. His career has crossed manufacturing and academia, including roles in skilled-trades education, college instruction, educational technology, and secondary-school teaching.

Together, Mike and Kristen write and speak about life after alcohol, rebuilding trust, parenting through uncertainty, and creating families and organizations where belonging does not require anyone to disappear.
